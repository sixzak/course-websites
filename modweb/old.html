Weekly preparation from start to midterm

Week (2/22)
Choose your 4 person group and tell your class leader. You must have your groups chosen before the start of next week's class, so I can distribute the acting materials and we can start learning the material.

Week (2/29)
For next week, download the midterm acting scriptView and use your group's part (1,2,3,4,5 or 6).
You must find the character (role) and the modern meaning for each line in your group's section.
ex. Good Hamlet, cast thy nighted colour off
copy that line, open your browser search engine (google) paste the line and write "nofear" (from no fear Shakespeare - Sparknotes).
Then from the No Fear Shakespeare - Sparknotes page, find the character and modern meaning and copy/paste the information on your group's script.

Gertrude: Good Hamlet, cast thy nighted colour off
My dear Hamlet, stop wearing these black clothes, (highlight)

Divide the work with your 4 person group and finish this by next week's class, so we can start using our acting scripts.

Week (3/08)
Almost all groups have completed their scripts with roles and meanings.
Before next class, your group must divide the roles between your group members. Do this in a fair way (each member should have about the same number of lines as other members)
Each group will show me who will learn which line of the script.
This must be done before next week, so you can start memorizing the Shakespeare lines (not the meaning lines - meaning is just to help you understand the Shakespeare lines).
Keep reading your section with your group (not just in our class).
Take a look at the BBC Animated Tales - Hamlet and watch your section (see where the actors are, listen to their emotions).
Take a look at the midterm exam questions (you should already know some answers).

Week (3/14)
We did this in class to start speaking lines from Hamlet and to understand the story better 15 minute hamlet.
For next week, practice saying your lines from your group's section of Hamlet everyday. Your goal is to memorize at least 1/2 of your lines by next week - memorize at least one line everyday (just keep saying it over and over again, then the next day work on the next line)
Your class should think about the answers to the questions on the Midterm exam page (your should know the answers to the first 11 questions by now.)

Week (3/21)
We did this in class to show emotion Insults in Hamlet.
For next week, practice saying your lines from your group's section of Hamlet everyday. Your goal is to memorize ALL of your lines by next week (you really need to try because it isn't easy and you need to memorize your lines before we start to act)
Your class should find the answers to the questions on the Midterm exam page (your should know the answers to the first 14 questions by now.)

Week (3/28)
For next class, make sure everyone in your group has a paper printout of the script (try to shorten it like I showed you in class - no meanings, combine the lines for each role).

Learn the answers to the midterm exam questions. Don't spend too much time doing this - just read them quickly and say the answers.

Practice your own lines every day (read say, don't read say). You will have to have all of your lines memorized so we can start acting. Watch the video of your group's part (you can listen to the voices) and try to understand the movement (on stage - off stage) and where your scenes are.

Week (4/4)
Monday class had a holiday. Thursday class covered material.
To prepare for next class, keep watching the BBC Animated Tales - Hamlet and make sure you have ALL lines memorized (and bring in your one page scripts).
Practice by yourself and with your group as much as possible.

Week (4/11)
Next week will be your written midterm exam. Know all 16 answers to the questions on the Midterm exam web page. Also, look at the files on that web page and try to learn more. Look at the slides from the powerpoints and think about what questions might be on the exam.
However, don't spend too much time on extra questions until you know all 16 answers well and can perform your acting well.
After the exam, if you think your group's acting is perfect (looking at the audience when speaking, having gestures, speaking with emotion) you may perform your acting section.
Watch your own group carefully, and help your group members. Make your acting interesting to watch. If you need more time to make your acting perfect, you can have one more week to practice.

1 After Laertes talks to Ophelia about Hamlet, he goes to ...

11 What are rosemary and violets for?
12 Whose skull does the gravedigger give to Hamlet?
13 How much does Hamlet say that he loved Ophelia?
14 Near the end, what does Hamlet tell Horatio to do?
15 Why does Ophelia first think that Hamlet is crazy?
16 Ophelia says at her father's head and heels are ____
