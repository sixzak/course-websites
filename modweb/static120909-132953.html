Week (5/16)
2 kinds of homework for next week:
1 Hamlet Themes
Discuss with your small group and bring some examples to class. Try to think of three examples for the theme A,B,C or D that your group is responsible for (think by yourself, then discuss with your group ). What do people do or what happens to them that shows the theme (think about individual characters ex. Hamlet, Ophelia, Claudius, etc).
A. The Impossibility of Certainty
The characters always doubt things about their lives and each other. What are they unsure of?
B. The Complexity of Action
Things are not as simple as they seem, and plans do not always work out the way they are designed. What are some things that don't happen as planned?
C. The Mystery of Death
How does Hamlet think about living and dying? What happens to make him think about this theme?
D. The Nation as a Diseased Body
What is the relationship of Denmark to its rulers?
How does the "health" of Denmark change?

You only need to think of examples for the theme that you were given in class. These themes were taken from Sparknotes.

2. All lines for your play should be memorized. Start practicing acting your play with your group. Practice a few scenes every day. Watch the animated tales video again and make sure your group is looking at the audience, speaking with emotion and using gestures for every sentence. Also, start thinking if there is anything you want to add to your play to make it more personal or interesting.

2) Next week, you will also have to show me that you have all of the answers to the Final exam. You should start learning them, but they don't have to be memorized, yet. You do need to bring the answers to class (Everyone). Final written exam will be on 6/6 for the Monday class, the Thursday class will need to chose another date for the exam.
3) Homework:
Download this file One Minute Hamlet ↓ ↓viewview and act the part number I told you in class. You only have to act those words - with lots of feeling and useful gestures . (It's called One Minute Hamlet because all 15 lines can be acted in a little over one minute - about 1.5 minutes).
Most parts have only two characters (the other people in the group can choose any part to act)
Everyone needs to be in at least one video.
Read the moodle explanation - and upload the video.
