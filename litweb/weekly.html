Important
Presentation format (PowerPoint) - total time ~5 minutes:
1) Story name (title)
Your group English names and numbers
2) Author name, picture (if you can find it), birth and death years (ex. 1908 - 1993)
One or two interesting or important sentences about the author (not about his or her writing)
3) Main Characters and 3 personality adjectives for each (try to use words you understand)
4) Setting (place/places and time)
5) Two themes - use your own words (do not search the internet)
*these five slides should take less than 2 minutes to present (students can share presenting this information - reading is OK for these 5 parts only)
6) "conversation/acting" from the story. Can be groups of two students or one student at a time. Students in the same group should memorize different parts of the story. (all students in the group will do the conversation/acting - this must be memorized)
*you must put the conversations on the PowerPoint slides (each conversation may need more than one slide - don't make the words too small for your classmates to read)
*you may not read the slide - it is for the audience to read while you speak
*try to do your conversation/acting with emotion and gestures

How many sentences? Try to do at least 2 important ideas for each page of the story. The ideas can be short conversations or important memorized sentences.
Try to choose important sentences that show main ideas of the story. After the audience hears your story sentences, they should be able to understand the story plot.
*this sentences/conversations/acting section should not take more than 3 minutes

Week 1/04
This week we went over all of the answers to the NEW questions.

Next week January 12th is the final written exam. You must know all of the answers well - also think about key words. Study by yourself then have a friend ask you all of the questions - see if you know the answers. There will also be questions that we have not covered in class, but don't even think about them until you know all of answers that we have discussed in class.

In addition, you must also know themes for 3 different stories and be able to explain them. This means:
1) Write the story name
2) Write the theme. Remember a theme is the idea behind the story - a main point the author is trying to make about life. Use your own words, do not mention the characters or the plot details.
3) Write a detail from the story plot that shows/explains the theme. This could be a few sentences that talk about what characters do. This should have a relationship to the theme (it should demonstrate the theme).

Which 3 stories? Any 3, but they must all be different.
