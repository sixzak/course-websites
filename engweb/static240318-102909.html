Haiku

desert stars
I end all thought
of moderation

dusk
where wild apples
have rolled to a stop

my nephew
doesn't let me hold his hand
first day of spring

roadside puddle
a street dog
licks the winter moon

a bee chose
the rose I meant to pluck . . .
empty vase

early morning sun
scattered on the table
several grains of salt

...

on the piano
photos of the ones
who don't visit

crossroads
the wind takes my hair
the other way

3 a.m.
the overhead fan
clicks clicks clicks

weekend over
rattle of a plastic soldier
in my guitar

daffodils
a little girl practicing
her princess wave

waiting for the hurricane
another bend
in the checkout line
