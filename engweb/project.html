Project - has 2 separate parts: an in-class presentation and a video:
Think about last semesters' presentations and videos- how can you do them better and more effectively?

In-class Presentation (PowerPoint) containing the following sections:
1) Character introductions (short - just a few words to know the relationships between the characters)
2) Brief summary (general only about the 2+ minute acting - not about the parts you don't act in class)
3) Acting without speaking - each member acts a sentence (at least 6 words) from the 2+ minute acting. - words must be on PowerPoint slide for audience to see, and another group member(s) will say the sentence while the acting member acts with gestures, but without speaking
4) Acting (2+ minutes). Make sure to look at the audience when speaking (look and react to other actors when not speaking), show and speak with emotion, use meaningful gestures. (all group members must act)
5) Theme and Explanation (make sure theme and explanation are from the part(s) you act in-class).
6 Haiku and short explanation- Haiku - about one event from the acting - 3 lines (don't use character names). Explanation - what do the characters do? How do they feel?
* Total presentation should be about 5-7 minutes . Use images - practice speaking loudly and clearly, Think about what you will say, which images and what text you will use on each slide*

Video about 5 minutes (about 5 minutes of dialogue) will be uploaded to YouTube with a link on Tronclass. Total video must be memorized (do not read lines), this can easily be done by practicing and recording the video in small sections, then edit them together.
If your video acting even looks like it is not memorized, your video grade will be a failing grade.
Make sure to show your emotion (face and voice)and use gestures. You can act by looking at a fixed camera or have someone to move/guide the camera.
** of course your video will be more effective if you don't wear masks (better pronunciation, more emotion), but if a group member really wants to wear a mask - he/she should have at least one line of acting without a mask (perhaps a close-up without other actors).
